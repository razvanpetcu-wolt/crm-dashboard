guideHow to read incrementality results

Incrementality measures whether a CRM campaign caused additional orders, GOV and users — or whether those customers would have purchased anyway without the campaign. We compare a treatment group (received the campaign) vs a control group (did not). A positive result means the campaign drove real uplift. A negative result means the control group outperformed — the campaign may have disrupted natural behaviour.

✅ Positive
Inc. GOV > €1,000. The campaign drove real additional orders and revenue beyond what would have happened organically. Scale or repeat.
⚠️ Mixed
Inc. GOV between -€1,000 and +€1,000, or conflicting signals (e.g. positive orders but negative GOV). Inconclusive — monitor closely before scaling.
❌ Negative
Inc. GOV < -€1,000. Control group outperformed treatment — the campaign likely cannibalised organic orders or caused audience fatigue. Pause and investigate.

Measurement windows — all measured from campaign start date:

1. In Campaign
Impact measured while the campaign is still actively running.
2. Post 7 days
Early signal — incremental impact within the first 7 days from campaign start.
3. Post 28 days
Medium-term read — incremental impact within 28 days. Typically the primary evaluation window.
4. Post 56 days
Longer-term view — checks whether early lift was sustained or if cannibalisation emerged over time.

⚠️ Note: There is no fixed GOV threshold to define pass/fail. Results should be read in context — compare windows across the same campaign and benchmark against similar past campaigns. A campaign positive at 28d but negative at 56d warrants further investigation.
